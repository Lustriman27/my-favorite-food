Nasi Goreng

Back To Home


Description

Nasi Goreng is a popular Indonesian dish that translates to "fried rice" in English. It is a flavorful and aromatic fried rice dish, typically made with leftover cooked rice and a combination of vegetables, meat, seafood, or tofu. The key to its deliciousness lies in the combination of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked white rice (preferably chilled)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 red chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 200g cooked and diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 2 tablespoons regular soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or more soy sauce if you prefer)
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp paste (terasi) or 1/2 teaspoon shrimp paste powder (optional, but adds depth of flavor)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Fried eggs and prawn crackers (krupuk) for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and red chilies. Stir-fry until the onions become translucent and the mixture becomes fragrant.
  3. If using meat or seafood, add it to the wok and cook until it's heated through and lightly browned. If you're using tofu, you can add it at this point or later in the process.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables to the wok and continue stir-frying for a few minutes until they are slightly cooked but still crisp.
  5. Push the ingredients to one side of the wok and add a little more oil to the empty space. Crack the eggs into the empty space and scramble them until they are cooked.
  6. Add the chilled cooked rice to the wok and break up any clumps using a spatula or fork. Mix the rice with the other ingredients and stir-fry for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  7. Pour in the sweet soy sauce, regular soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and fish sauce. If using shrimp paste or shrimp paste powder, add it now. Toss everything together until the rice is evenly coated with the sauces and seasonings.
  8. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper according to your taste.
  9. Continue stir-frying for a few more minutes until the rice is thoroughly heated and has absorbed the flavors of the sauces and spices.
  10. Stir in the sliced green onions and remove the wok from the heat.
  11. Serve the Nasi Goreng hot, garnished with fried eggs and prawn crackers if desired.
